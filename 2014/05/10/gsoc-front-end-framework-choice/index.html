--- title: "[GSoC] Front-End framework choice" kind: article created_at: 2014-05-10 11:50:00 UTC author: Maciej (Developer Website) image: https://avatars1.githubusercontent.com/u/2752578?s=50 categories: GSoC-2014 tags: "news developers-page ruby jekyll" layout: post ---Today I had to choose which front-end framework I would like to use in Tim Videos project.

Today's choice is really huge and that choice make decision even harder, but on the other hand with every modern front-end framework you will get the same results and features: responsive design, fully customization of colors, shapes, etc. grid system, icons, customized forms, buttons, etc.

I have some experience with 2 front-end frameworks: Bootrap and Pure CSS, and I think I will choose one of them, but I made some research to find some alternatives and make a choice.

Alex Ivanovs wrote on Flippin' Awesome blog great post about Bootstrap alternatives: http://flippinawesome.org/2014/02/17/8-bootstrap-alternatives/. I recommend to read it and comments below, because it is a great source of information about modern front-end frameworks. I also recommend to look at spreadsheet of Styleguide & Boilerplate Patterns from Tyler Sticka (Cloud Four) with comparison of most popular front-end frameworks and its features: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheet/ccc?key=0AiN0QfBTPpOCdDFjWlM0eU1ra21XanZkekxGbjA2WWc.

I did not make a choice yet, currently I am preparing some mockups of website, after that I will choose framework that suits all my needs.
